# *Botryllus schlosseri* Nickel Toxicity

## Background

*B. schlosseri* is a cosmopolitain marine tunicate that undergoes weekly blasotgeneis, a form of asexual budding.
<img width="2550" height="2401" alt="Simple Blastogenic Stage Schematic" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/01793516-44ab-4066-9ecc-360eee76e9f6" />

Nickel toxicity has never been characterized in *B. schlosseri*. Here we perform several experments evaluating nickel impacts on the transcriptomic and proteomic profiles of this marine tunicate. We also establish the LC50 of this species.


## Objectives 

a)  Determine nickel LC50 for _B. schlosseri_ in full strength artifical seawater.

b)  Evaluate the superoxide dismutase 1 (SOD1) activity response of _B. schlosseri_ in response to nickel.

c)  Evaluate the impact of nickel on expression of genes relating to oxidative stress, senescence, stemness, and apoptosis.
